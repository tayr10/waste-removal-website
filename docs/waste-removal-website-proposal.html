Required Pages (Minimum Viable Site)

  1. Home

    Hero with CTA (Get a Free Quote), services overview, trust badges (licensed/insured), customer reviews pull, service area map, click-to-call.

  2. Services

    Individual sub-pages for each service category: residential cleanout, commercial removal, appliance hauling, yard waste, furniture removal. Each page = keyword target.

  3. About

    Who you are, founding story, commitment to eco-friendly disposal, photos of your team/truck. Builds trust for a local business.

  4. Pricing / Get a Quote

    Even a "starting from $X" range reduces friction. Embed a contact/quote form. Consider a photo-upload estimator (Wix Form or Jotform embed).

  5. Service Area

    List towns/cities you serve, embed Google Maps. Critical for local SEO—Google reads this page for geographic relevance.

  6. Contact

    Phone (click-to-call), email, embedded form, business hours, address if applicable. Keep this one tap away on mobile.

Claude's Role in the Build

Claude outputs SEO-optimized copy, meta titles, meta descriptions, FAQ sections, and even complete HTML/CSS pages if using a code-based build.

Claude can also generate a complete robots.txt, sitemap structure, alt text for images, Google Business Profile description, and review request templates—reducing vendor costs to near zero.

Risks: 16-Year-Old Owning the End-to-End Process

This section is written directly to or for the 16-year-old owner. These risks are real and manageable—but only if addressed upfront. Ignoring them can result in accounts suspended, contracts voided, or funds frozen.

Bottom line: A 16-year-old can operate every aspect of this website day-to-day, but a parent or guardian must be the legal signatory on all platform accounts, the domain registration, the payment processor, and any business registrations. This is not a workaround—it is the legally correct structure.

⚖️

Contract Incapacity

In most U.S. states, minors cannot enter into binding contracts. Any platform Terms of Service (Wix, Hostinger, Google, Stripe, PayPal) signed by a minor can be voided. This could mean account suspension or financial holds.

✓ Mitigation: All accounts registered and signed under a parent/guardian's name. Minor manages operations; adult is the legal owner.
High Risk
🏦

Payment Processing & Bank Account

Stripe, Square, and PayPal require business accounts that comply with KYC (Know Your Customer) laws. A minor cannot legally open a business bank account in the U.S. Funds can be frozen if age is discovered post-registration.

✓ Mitigation: Open a business bank account and payment processor account in a parent/guardian's name. Minor handles daily management, not account ownership.
High Risk
🏢

Business Entity / LLC Formation

Some states (OR, IL, CO, MN) do not permit minors to own businesses. Even in permissive states (TX, CA, DE), an adult must typically co-sign LLC formation paperwork. Without proper structure, all liability falls personally on the minor (and their family).

✓ Mitigation: Form as a sole proprietorship or LLC with a parent as co-owner. Consult a local business attorney or use a service like Bizee ($50–100) for guidance.
High Risk
📋

Customer Contracts & Service Agreements

Any service contract or quote confirmation signed by a minor is voidable by the minor—meaning a customer or supplier could use this to dispute payment, void a service agreement, or refuse to pay.

✓ Mitigation: Have an attorney draft a simple 1-page service agreement. Parent/guardian signs as the contracting party. Use a digital signature tool (HelloSign free tier).
Medium Risk
💰

Tax Obligations

Any minor's business earning more than $400 must file and pay self-employment taxes. This applies even if the income goes into a parent's account. Failing to file creates IRS penalties that follow the family.

✓ Mitigation: Track all income and expenses from Day 1 in a spreadsheet (Claude can create a template). File a Schedule SE / Schedule C with your federal taxes annually.
Medium Risk
🌐

Platform Age Requirements

Wix, Hostinger, and Google all require users to be 13+ to use their services, but business/payment features require age 18+. Accounts discovered to be under-18-owned can be suspended without notice, taking the website down.

✓ Mitigation: Register all accounts with the parent/guardian's email. The 16-year-old gets a sub-account or editor-level access on platforms that support it (Wix supports multi-user roles).
Medium Risk
📜

Business License / Permits

Many municipalities require a local business license for waste hauling operations (varies by state and city). A minor cannot apply for most business licenses on their own—and operating without a required license creates legal exposure.

✓ Mitigation: Check your city/county website for "home occupation permit" and "junk hauler license" requirements. Parent applies. Budget $25–150 for permits.
Medium Risk
🚛

Insurance & Liability

A waste removal business has real physical liability (damage to customer property, personal injury). A 16-year-old cannot take out general liability insurance or commercial auto insurance in their own name.

✓ Mitigation: Parent/guardian obtains general liability ($500–1,200/year) and commercial auto coverage before any job is performed. This also builds customer trust (display "fully insured" on website).
Low Web Risk

The good news: A 16-year-old can absolutely operate this business day-to-day—manage the website, handle customer communications, run social media, generate quotes, and grow the brand. The legal structure just needs to sit correctly above them with a parent/guardian as the formal entity. Many successful teen entrepreneurs have launched exactly this way.
